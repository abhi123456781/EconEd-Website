About EconEd

We're on a mission to make economic education accessible to all students.

Our Story

EconEd was founded with a simple yet powerful vision: to bridge the digital divide for seniors in our communities. We recognized that as technology continues to advance rapidly, many seniors are being left behind, unable to access essential services, connect with loved ones, or enjoy the benefits of the digital world.

What started as a small group of volunteers teaching basic computer skills at a local community center has grown into a nationwide movement with chapters across the country. Our dedicated team of volunteers works tirelessly to create accessible, engaging, and practical learning experiences for seniors.

Today, EconEd continues to expand its reach, bringing digital literacy to more seniors every day and empowering them to navigate the digital world with confidence and independence.

Our Values

The core principles that guide our work and mission.

Equal Access

We believe in equal access opportunities that inspire the next generation of leaders. We are a diverse, equitable, and inclusive community where all members feel respected.

Authentic Leadership

We see diversity and inclusion as connected to our mission and critical to ensuring the well-being of our members and the community that we serve.

Transparent Communication

We practice and encourage transparent communication in all interactions, including those at our board meetings, forums, educational seminars, and community initiatives.

Cultural Competency

We pursue cultural competency by creating diverse learning opportunities at our events and analyze quantitative and qualitative data to evaluate potential issues.

Systemic Awareness

We advocate for and support thinking as to how systemic inequities impact our organization's work, and how best to address that in a way that is consistent with our mission.

Inclusive Community

We value diverse life experiences and heritages and ensure that all voices are heard. We challenge assumptions about what it takes to be a strong leader.
